Set Expectations

Whenever a new task is identified, which requires work, then we (Software Research team) try to ensure we understand not only the problem, but also the stakeholders, and their expectations. As part of this process we construct a 1-2 page summary detailing: Background, Stakeholders, Expectations, Timeframe.  This summary is then communicated and agreed with the Stakeholders.

Without explicity stating the expectations (be it a document, a presentation, a prototype), nobody involved (including the stakeholders) is aware of what to expect until the final deliverable is available - which is often too late, especially if it doesn’t meet their needs.

The old adage “if you don’t know where you are going, how will you get there?” is never more true.

The identification of expectations is important across many more areas: * Emails * Meetings * Whiteboard sessions

All too often, these forms of communication decend into a pile of words, and no actions.

Examples: * Response required before end of thursday 12th 2009. * Please can you review from a customers perspective. * …

It is a hard thing to do, but at the end of your emails (or perhaps at the start), set some expectations, see what happens.
